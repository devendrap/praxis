Carleton examines extreme wealth inequality in the AI era, noting that Elon Musk's estimated net worth ($786 billion in 2026) exceeds the combined wealth of the top ten billionaires from 2015. She highlights a disconnect between economic growth and job creation, with GDP growing at 4.4% in Q3 2025 while employment remained essentially flat.

## The Problem

AI-driven technological change concentrates wealth among those controlling the technology while creating labor market instability. Recent college graduate unemployment reached nearly 10% by December 2025, and average job search duration extended to 11 weeks. Meanwhile, the bottom 50% of the global population owns just 2% of total wealth.

## The Proposed Solution

Carleton proposes establishing a pooled public fund from government tax revenue generated by AI companies, similar to Alaska's Permanent Fund. Citizens would receive equal annual distributions from this fund, providing economic security without creating dependency on those controlling AI development.

## Implementation Considerations

The proposal requires careful deliberation on several practical questions: What qualifies as an "AI company"? Should policies be implemented at state or national levels? How would the fund's sustainability be ensured as AI markets evolve?

## Caveats

Carleton emphasizes this approach should complement other redistributive policies like Universal Basic Income rather than replacing them. She acknowledges uncertainty about whether distributions could substitute for full-time income but argues that even modest payments could provide meaningful relief for struggling families.
