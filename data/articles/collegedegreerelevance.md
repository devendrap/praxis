Carleton examines how educational institutions can help students prepare for an AI-driven labor market, arguing that colleges must modernize to remain relevant rather than becoming obsolete due to technological change.

## The Trust Crisis

Students increasingly question college's value proposition. While a college degree historically promised career advancement and earnings potential, automation threatens entry-level positions. Carleton notes that "just 41% of young adults believed that higher education is very important" in 2023, down from 74% a decade earlier.

## Life Expectancy Gap

Research by Case and Deaton (2023) shows the mortality gap between college graduates and non-graduates widened from 2.6 years in 1992 to 8.5 years in 2021, suggesting education correlates with longevity benefits beyond earnings.

## Proposed Solutions

Carleton identifies three institutional responses:

1. **Curriculum Modernization**: Professors should connect coursework to real-world applications. Rather than dismissing essay writing as obsolete, educators can demonstrate how humans must interpret AI outputs critically.

2. **Career Preparation Support**: Career planning shouldn't wait until senior year. Mandatory seminars on networking, recruiter communication, and application strategy help level the playing field for underprivileged students.

3. **Network Leverage**: Elite institutions benefit from alumni networks that provide hidden job opportunities. Universities should formalize these connections through centralized platforms and career services databases.

## The Inequality Problem

Carleton emphasizes that network advantages aren't equally distributed. Elite college attendance increases chances of reaching top earnings by 50% versus flagship institutions, and first-job transitions explain nearly two-thirds of earnings gaps between low and high-income students.

## Conclusion

Colleges cannot control technological change, but they can ensure fewer students "fall through the cracks" through intentional institutional support.
